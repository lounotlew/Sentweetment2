What is Sentweetment 2?

Sentweetment 2 is a Django web application for performing sentiment analysis on Twitter accounts (users). Using the VADER Lexicon, Sentweetment 2 tokenizes each tweet made by a user (for a max. of latest 200 tweets, limited by the Twitter API) to individual words, and applies a polarity score to each word. Then, these individual scores are aggregated per-tweet to give an overall tweet polarity score. These scores are also used to calculate an overall user polarity score (by their average), and ultimately the general sentiment of a Twitter account/user. All of these operations are performed through pandas.

What is a "Polarity Score"?

A polarity score is a number that tells us how "positive" or "negative" a word is, in the context of social media. These scores are determined by the lexicon mentioned above. A tweet's polarity score is the sum of that tweet's individual words' polarities, and a user's sentiment score is the average polarity of all the tweets that were analyzed (max. of latest 200 tweets).

Notes

- The methodology is based on UC Berkeley's Data Science 100 Project 1: "Trump, Twitter, and Text".

- Although the overall context of a tweet isn't taken into account (yet), the general idea is that the more positive/negative words a tweet contains, the more likely it is that that tweet is positive/negative.
